Dr. Denis Mukwege

The repairer of abused women

  • 1955 - Born in Bukavu, Democratic Republic of Congo.
  • 1983 - He obtained medical degree, and then he took his first professional steps at Lemera hospital, located south of Bukavu.
  • 1984 - He obtained a scholarship from the Swedish Pentecostal Mission to follow a specialization in gynecology at the University of Angers, in France.
  • 1996 - During the First Congo War, his hospital was brutally destroyed during the attack on Lemera. Mukwege escapes death while several patients and nurses are murdered. He takes refuge in Nairobi, then decides to return to Congo. With the help of the PMU (Pingstmissionens Utvecklingssamarbete, a Swedish charitable association), he founded the Panzi hospital in Bukavu. He specializes in caring for female victims of sexual assault, providing them with medical, psychological, economic and legal assistance.
  • 2010 - He is recognized as one of the world's specialists in the treatment of fistulas; as such, he received, among other things, two university distinctions.
  • 2012 - He is the victim of an attack in the center of Bukavu. The guardian of his house is shot dead after alerting him to danger, his car is set on fire and Mukwege is tied up. But thanks to the intervention of local residents, who came to his aid, he came out safe and sound. He then took refuge in Belgium for a few months before returning to work in Congo-Kinshasa.
  • 2015 - He became a doctor of medical sciences at the Free University of Brussels after defending a thesis entitled Etiology, classification and treatment of traumatic uro-genital and lower genito-digestive fistulas in eastern Democratic Republic of Congo.
  • 2018 - He receives the Nobel Peace Prize with Nadia Murad, for their efforts to put an end to the use of sexual violence as a weapon of war.
  • 2020 - During the Council of Ministers, the President of the Republic instructed the government to ensure his safety and to open an investigation into the death threats against the doctor who “repairs” the women. He lives under surveillance inside his hospital, having been the victim of several attempted murders.
